Portal Property Page

Portals are one-way doorways to other rooms; they transport avatars between the current room and another room in the same world. You can specify the room to connect to and the portal for returning to this room. You can also set the entry point for avatars to be placed in the new room, and the direction in which the avatars will face on entering.

The default appearance of the portal object is an open door with a blue sky and desert setting beyond the doorway.

Property Page Options

Portal connect to

Room

Choose the room in the world to which you want this portal to connect. You can choose (Not Set) if you want to set the portal's destination room later; this is useful if you have not built the destination room yet.

Portal

Choose the returning portal in the destination room that will help the avatars come back to this spot. If a destination room does not have any portals in it, you must teleport to that room and create some. Then select this portal again and re-set the destination and the return portal.

If an avatar enters the room through this portal

Entry point

This is the entry point at which all avatars appear after passing through a portal. It is a fixed location in the destination room. This is very useful if your room is designed to focus on the view from a certain position. X, Y, and Z represent the coordinates of this point. The X-axis runs left and right, the Y-axis runs up and down, and the Z-axis runs into and out of the screen.

Facing vector

This is the direction you want the avatars to face when they arrive in the destination room by going through the portal. Use this if your portal's entry point is near a wall or pillar, and you want the avatars to see the whole room instead. If you set the three components of this vector to (1, 0, 0), avatars entering the room face in the direction of the positive X-axis.

Scatter around entry point

When many avatars travel through portals to the same entry point in a room, the avatars tend to stack all into the same space. To prevent this, you can establish a scatter zone around the entry point, so the avatars do not arrive at exactly the same spot.

A unit is equal to three feet.
