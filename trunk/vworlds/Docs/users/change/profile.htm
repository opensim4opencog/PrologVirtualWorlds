What are Profiles?

Each avatar in a virtual world is represented by a graphical image and a profile. The profile is seen when you click on that avatar for more details. Your avatar has a profile as well, and you can use that profile to change any of the following information:

  • Appearance
  • Description
  • Details
  • Friends
  • Ignored

Appearance

You can choose an avatar to represent you in the world from the list of prepared avatars. Once you choose an avatar, you can test the gestures and poses of the avatar with the buttons. The avatar you currently have in the world is not updated until you click OK.

Description

This is one of the attributes that other avatars can see if they look at your profile. You can type in text, describing yourself or anything you want to say about yourself.

Details

Details include personal information about yourself, such as where you live, your gender, your real name, age, email alias and web page. You don't have to provide any information that you are not comfortable with. You can set the individual viewing privileges for each detail, as well, to be seen by the public (everyone), only your friends (avatars in the Friends list), or only yourself (private).

Friends

You can save your favorite avatars' names in a Friends List in your own Profile. Friends can see information in your Profile that regular people cannot, if you set that information to the Friends level. For example, if you didn't want your age or location to be seen by all on your avatar's Details page, you could set the age and location fields to be visible only to Friends. Everyone else would not see age or location.

Ignored

When an avatar is added to your Ignore List, that avatar graphic appears to be a cat stuffed in a bottle (to you.) You no longer see their messages and the room hears a message that you are ignoring that particular avatar. Other avatars can still "hear" the messages from the avatar you are ignoring.
