Moving Your Avatar

The advantage to three-dimensional worlds is that you can move in three dimensions. You can navigate your avatar forwards, backwards, left and right, but also up and down. The view of the world changes as you move your avatar into rooms, around tables, out the door, by the tree, or up in the air. You can turn in place by turning left or right, to see the panoramic view of the world.

If your camera angle is set to behind you, you might see through walls to see over your avatar's shoulder.

Moving with the Mouse

To move your avatar with the mouse:

  1. In Microsoft Virtual Worlds, click and hold in the graphical view of the world.
  2. "Push" the mouse forward or back, left or right, and your avatar moves inside of the world.

To "fly" or move upwards or downwards with the mouse:

  1. With the mouse: Click the graphical view of the world and hold down the CTRL key.
  2. Drag the mouse upwards or downwards to move your avatar.

    Note: You can not fly in a world if the gravity is on. Check with the world owner to see if gravity is on or off.

Moving with the Keyboard

To move your avatar with the keyboard:

  1. In Microsoft Virtual Worlds, click (and release) in the graphical view of the world.
  2. Press the arrow keys in the direction you want to move your avatar.

To "fly" or move upwards or downwards with the keyboard:

  1. With the mouse: Click (and release) in the graphical view of the world.
  2. Hold down the CTRL key.
  3. Press the arrow keys to move your avatar upwards or downwards.

    Note: You can not fly in a world if the gravity is on. Check with the world owner to see if gravity is on or off.
