Talking to People

In Microsoft Virtual Worlds, you can speak by typing your words into a window and then pressing Enter. Before you press Enter, you can change your mind or edit the words in the Chat window. After you press Enter, all the avatars in the same room as you can see your message. Avatars in other rooms will not see your "spoken" text.

If you are speaking to an avatar, they do not have to be in your avatar's direct line of sight to receive your message. Distance between avatars within the same room does not matter.

You can read the messages from other avatars in the Chat window. This window, or history of the conversation, starts recording the minute you step into the room. You can scroll up to see previous statements, emotions, and other messages from other avatars, back to the point where you entered the room.

To speak to others in the room:

  1. In the Chat Text area, type your message.
  2. Press ENTER, or click

The text you typed appears in the Chat History window.

Example:

If an avatar named Jane types in: Try taking the letter.

Virtual Worlds displays in the Chat History window:

Jane says, "Try taking the letter."
