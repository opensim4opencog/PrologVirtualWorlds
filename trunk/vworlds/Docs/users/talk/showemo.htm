Showing Emotions

During a conversation in Microsoft Virtual Worlds, you may want to show emotion without using the gesture buttons, by "posing" an action near your name in the Chat window. You can do this by typing in text to be added after your name, but not spoken aloud.

You can also do gestures, which animate your avatar with a series of images.

To show emotion/action in the Chat window:

  1. Type in the text that you want added after your name.
  2. Click the Emote button.

    You can type in an emotion or posture text in the chat box and click the gesture button of your choice. Your emotion or posture text appears next to your name as your avatar performs the gesture.

Example of Showing Emotion

Jane wants to simulate poking Jim in the ribs. She can't actually poke Jim in the ribs; but she can use emoting to fake it by putting an action statement after her name in the Chat window.

Jane types:

pokes Jim in the ribs.

Then she clicks the Emote button. Virtual Worlds displays:

Jane pokes Jim in the ribs.
